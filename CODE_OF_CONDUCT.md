# OpenCompliance Code of Conduct

OpenCompliance work should be rigorous, respectful, and explicit about uncertainty.

## Expected behavior

- Critique claims, code, proofs, and documents directly.
- Distinguish proved facts, attestations, judgment, and aspiration.
- Keep review comments technical and specific.
- Protect privacy and keep customer or sensitive material out of public repos.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation.
- Personal attacks or bad-faith pile-ons.
- Knowingly overstating maturity, proof coverage, or review status.
- Publishing private, customer, or security-sensitive data.

## Scope

This applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, and other project spaces in the OpenCompliance Foundation public org.

## Reporting

Until a dedicated foundation contact address exists, raise non-sensitive conduct concerns in the governance repo or contact an OpenCompliance Foundation organisation owner privately through GitHub for sensitive issues.
